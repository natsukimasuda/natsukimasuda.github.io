about me.

My name is Natsuki Masuda. My best friends describe me as extroverted, inventive and adaptable. My hobbies include reading, travelling, filming and listening to people talk whether it be in person or TED talks. My go-to method to get to know a friend is through his or her favorite music and book. I am currently reading “Predictably Irrational” by Dan Ariely. outside the classroom.

extracurricular sports.

four year varsity athelete

Throughout my high school career, I took on four different seasonal sports – volleyball, basketball, soccer and softball. Playing sports was a great medium to meet new friends outside the classroom. We practice two hours a day, three times a week and attend local and international competitions. I got to travel with my team to compete in different countries such as Hong Kong, China and Vietnam. As a team, we learned how to turn the heartbreak of defeat into motivation to become better. The lessons I learned about teamwork and communication are invaluable. By the end, we were family.

model united nations.

security council

Model United Nations is an extracurricular activity that simulates conferences held at United Nations. We meet for two hours on Tuesdays through Thursdays to discuss resolutions that are based on real world issues such as Non-Proliferation of weapons of mass destruction. We debate on the behalf of a nation’s delegation to pass resolutions that align with their interests. I undergo prior research in order to accurately represent a nation, and this deepens my involvement in current events. Throughout the sessions, I have represented countries like Italy and Korea to explore global issues through various cultural lenses. MUN has been a big step to becoming the kind of individual I aspire to be in the future. As mentioned in my video, I will travel to Beijing this March to attend an international MUN conference with over 800 students from 40 different schools to represent the United States at Security Council.

ritz-carlton osaka.

four week internship

During the summer before my senior year, I had the opportunity to intern at the club lounge at Ritz-Carlton Osaka. I set up tables, brewed coffee, greeted guests and assisted their check-ins. In the beginning of my internship, I was nervous and felt out of place. However, with time, I was able to adapt to my surroundings and act more comfortably around guests. The experience that was most valuable was the conversations with the guests. I talked to CEOs, environmental activists, designers, and entrepreneurs. By reaching out, I was able to learn about about their experiences and expertise. My internship was a memorable and lasting experience. At Keio University, I would love to pursue internships and actively seek opportunities. I intend to continue initiating conversation to learn with and through others.
